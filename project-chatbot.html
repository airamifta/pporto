Corpus on Academic Chatbot

An AI-Powered Assistant for Student Queries

Project Summary

This project focused on developing the foundational components of an AI chatbot tailored for an academic environment. The primary work involved structuring a domain-specific corpus from scratch to enhance the chatbot's Natural Language Understanding (NLU). The main objective was to create a system capable of accurately interpreting and responding to a wide range of student queries, from administrative questions to course-related information.

Key Information

  • Role: NLP Developer
  • Tools: Python, NLP, Corpus Development, Google Colab
  • Duration: 2024
  • Links: GitHub

The Process

1. Domain Research

Analyzed common student questions and academic information structures to define the chatbot's scope and required knowledge base.

2. Corpus Structuring

Designed and built a structured corpus, defining intents (e.g., 'find_schedule'), entities (e.g., 'course_name'), and response patterns.

3. NLU Development

Focused on improving Natural Language Understanding by creating training data and exploring techniques to handle query variations.

4. Prototyping

Developed a basic prototype in Google Colab to test the corpus and initial NLU capabilities with sample student queries.

Results & Learnings

Key Outcomes

The primary outcome was a well-structured, domain-specific corpus that serves as the knowledge backbone for the academic chatbot. This foundational work successfully enables the system to recognize a variety of student intents and extract key entities from their questions, paving the way for a more advanced dialogue management system.

Lessons Learned

This project highlighted that the quality of a chatbot is heavily dependent on the quality and structure of its corpus. I learned the critical importance of meticulous data collection and annotation for training a reliable NLU model. It also provided practical experience in the iterative process of defining intents and entities to cover a specific domain effectively.
